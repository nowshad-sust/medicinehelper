Marlox (Magaldrate)

Therapeutic Group: Gastro Intestinal Disorders Antiulcerant Antacid

Presentation

Each Marlox fast dissolving tablet contains Magaldrate (Hydroxy-magnesium Aluminate) USP 400 mg.

Descriptions

Marlox is a basic magnesium aluminium compound (i.e. Hydroxymagnesium Aluminate), which neutralizes gastric acid extraordinarily quickly without raising the pH above 5-6. It also decreases the activity of pepsin in gastric secretion.

Indications

Marlox is indicated to relieve symptoms of dyspepsia, heartburn, acid indigestion, sour stomach, gastroesophageal reflux, hiatal hernia and flatulence. It is also prescribed in hyperacidity associated with peptic ulcers, gastritis and esophagitis. Magaldrate may be given to children if necessary.

Dosage and Administration

1-3 Marlox tablets, after meals and at bed time or as directed by the physician.

Side Effects

Constipation and diarrhea may occur.

Contraindications

Magaldrate is contraindicated in patients with known hypersensitivity to magnesium and aluminium. It is also contraindicated in patients with impaired renal functions.

Use in Pregnancy and Lactation

Pregnant women: Magaldrate may be used in pregnancy if indicated however one should avoid excessive dosage. Lactating mother: Magaldrate may pass into breast milk but has not been reported to cause problem in nursing babies.
